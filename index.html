🔍 Key Observations

  • Significant volume drop in W2 — Incidents fell 15.7% from W1 (5,645) to W2 (4,761). Device impact fell even more sharply at -18.9%.
  • W3 showed a mild rebound — Incidents ticked back up 2.8% in W3. Warrants monitoring to ensure the W1 spike doesn’t recur.
  • W1 resolution rate was notably low at 87.5% — W2 and W3 hit 99.5–99.7%, a dramatic recovery suggesting a W1 backlog of unresolved incidents.
  • Resolution time improved consistently W1→W3 — from 3.97 hrs to 2.81 hrs, a 29% improvement. W4 at 2.85 hrs is within noise.
  • High device impact per incident — averages 23–25 devices each; even small counts represent major user disruption.
  • Size mix is stable — SMALL ~73–77%, MEDIUM ~21–26%, LARGE ~1.8–2.0% across all weeks. No structural severity shift.

⚠ Gaps & Improvement Opportunities

  • W1 resolution gap needs root-cause — 703 W1 incidents still ACTIVE (12.5% unresolved) vs <0.5% in W2–W3.
  • W3 volume uptick needs watching — the +2.8% rebound suggests improvement wasn’t structural; a weekly alert threshold would help.
  • LARGE incident count is small but high-risk — 64–103 LARGE incidents/week with ~24 devices each deserve a separate deep-dive.
  • W4 partial-day caveat — the 27.8% drop looks dramatic but Mar 6 is not a full day; check next Monday for a clean read.
  • No SLA or resolution target defined — without a benchmark (e.g. “MEDIUM must resolve within 2 hrs”), it’s impossible to classify weeks as pass/fail.
