🔍 Key Observations

  • Significant volume drop in W2 — Incidents fell 15.7% from W1 (5,645) to W2 (4,761). This was the biggest single-week improvement. Device impact fell even more sharply at -18.9%.
  • W3 showed a mild rebound — Incidents ticked back up 2.8% in W3, suggesting the W2 drop was not fully sustained. This warrants monitoring to ensure W1 spike doesn't recur.
  • W1 resolution rate was notably low at 87.5% — W2 and W3 hit 99.5–99.7%, a dramatic recovery. This suggests W1 had a backlog of unresolved incidents (703 still ACTIVE), possibly from a spike or operational issue.
  • Resolution time improved consistently W1→W3 — from 3.97 hrs to 2.81 hrs, a 29% improvement. W4 is marginally slower at 2.85 hrs, but this is within noise.
  • High device impact per incident — incidents average 23–25 devices each. These are high-impact events; even a small count represents major user disruption.
  • Size mix is stable — SMALL stays at ~73–77%, MEDIUM at ~21–26%, LARGE at ~1.8–2.0% across all weeks. No structural shift in the severity distribution.

⚠ Gaps & Improvement Opportunities

  • W1 resolution gap needs root-cause — 703 incidents from W1 are still ACTIVE (12.5% unresolved). This is significantly worse than W2–W3 (<0.5%). Understanding what caused this backlog is critical.
  • W3 volume uptick needs watching — the +2.8% rebound in W3 after a strong W2 drop suggests the improvement wasn't structural. If MEDIUM/LARGE incidents are driven by specific infrastructure issues, a weekly alert threshold would help catch early.
  • LARGE incident count is small but high-risk — 64–103 LARGE incidents per week with ~24 devices each deserve a separate deep-dive. Even a few extra LARGE incidents in a week can represent thousands of impacted users.
  • W4 partial-day caveat — the 27.8% drop in W4 looks dramatic but Mar 6 is not a full day. A full-week view next Monday will give a cleaner read on the genuine trend direction.
  • No SLA or resolution target defined — without a benchmark (e.g. "MEDIUM must resolve within 2 hrs"), it's impossible to classify weeks as pass/fail. Defining SLA tiers by bucket would sharpen the analysis significantly.
  • Partner-level concentration unknown — are the same few partners generating most of the MEDIUM/LARGE incidents? A partner drill-down would identify systemic vs. one-off contributors.
